Topic: Formula in Excel

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students should be able to:

  1. Define and create basic formulas in Excel
  2. Use aggregate functions like SUM, AVERAGE, COUNT to perform calculations in Excel

Teaching & Learning Activities

Starter 10 mins

Brainstorming:
Why is it better to use a formula instead of adding numbers manually

Teacher Activity Student Activity Time
Introductory Activity
  • Discuss automation benefits (accuracy, speed); project formula bar.
  • Demonstrate MIN/MAX on marks data.
  • Note "=" symbol and basic syntax.
  • Enter data and apply functions.
 15 mins
Activity 1
    Demonstrate:
  • Simple formula (e.g., =A1+B1), then SUM (=SUM(B2:B10)).

    • Guide practical:
  • Apply MIN/MAX to class marks.
  • Practice on provided marks dataset.
  • Follow and replicate steps.
 15 mins
Activity 2
  • Demonstration on basic functions and AutoFill.
  • Group discussion: Create simple family budget sheet..
  • Watch and replicate steps.
  • Collaborate and apply functions.
 15 mins

Lesson Closure 5 mins

    Recap:
  • How does SUM promote responsibility in data handling

Teaching & Learning Resources

  • Textbook: Elective ICT Manual
  • Whiteboard
  • Projector
  • Laptop
  • TLM 1 — Printed handout
  • MS Excel

Assessment Strategy

  • Oral Q&A: Teacher ask students questions and they answer. Students aak questions and the class or teacher answer.
  • Observation: Monitor group work participation

Post-Lesson Evaluation

Reflection:

  • Learners proud of using excel to that level.
  • Formulas in Excel understood well.
  • Some groups had errors in writing formulas.
Remarks:
  • Lesson objectives are largely achieved. Students were able to create formulas and use basic functions in Excel. More practice is needed for complex formulas.
